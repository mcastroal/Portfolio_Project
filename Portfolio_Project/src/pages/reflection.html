Onboarding Reflections

Professional Development

During onboarding, I realized how critical it is to actively seek feedback and treat it as a tool for growth rather than criticism. Working in a structured, professional environment taught me that soft skills like communication, time management, and accountability are just as important as technical skills. Moving forward, I plan to lean into mentorship opportunities, continue building confidence in professional settings, and refine how I present myself, both in person and online.

Technical Growth

One major realization I had during onboarding is that problem-solving is more important than knowing everything. I used to think that being a developer meant memorizing syntax, but I’ve come to understand that learning how to break down problems, research effectively, and ask the right questions is what truly matters. My goal is to deepen my understanding of all three programming languages, strengthen my debugging skills, and contribute to real-world projects that push me outside my comfort zone.

Personal Growth

Personally, this onboarding experience helped me recognize the importance of balancing ambition with self-care. It’s easy to get caught up in performance and forget to rest or celebrate small wins. I’ve learned that growth isn’t always linear, and that’s okay. Going forward, I want to continue exploring creative outlets like pottery and movement to stay grounded while I pursue my goals in tech.
